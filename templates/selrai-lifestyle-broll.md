# selrai-lifestyle-broll

**Use when**: you need a lifestyle still — your product in real-world context,
or you in your environment, that reads as authentic rather than studio.

## Recommended model

- **Higgsfield Soul 2.0** with `Soul Cinema` aesthetic preset.
- Fallback: **Photodump** for hyper-candid IG-story look.

## Prompt

```text
Wide environmental shot of <your-character> using <your-product> at a sunlit
kitchen counter, mid-morning soft golden light through gauze curtains, 35mm
narrative lens, natural skin tones, slight haze in the air, candid moment
caught between gestures, depth of field with foreground bokeh.
```

## Parameters

- **Aspect ratio**: `16:9` (b-roll) or `9:16` (vertical reel)
- **Quality**: `high`
- **Seed**: lock if you'll generate multiple b-roll moments in the same scene

## What to fill in

- `<your-character>` — Soul ID name or 8-word description.
- `<your-product>` — short product name.
- Replace `kitchen counter` with: home studio, garage workshop, open warehouse,
  city café table, beach blanket — pick the actual environment your audience
  associates with you.

## Variations to try once

1. Time of day: `late afternoon, sun streaming low through window, long shadows`
2. Camera angle: `low angle, looking up past the product at the subject's face`
3. Activity: `pouring coffee mid-conversation` instead of `using product`
4. Mood: `quiet focus, no eye contact with camera` instead of `candid moment`

## Pairing

- Stitch 5 of these into a single Reel via your editor — they read as a real
  day-in-the-life, not staged.
- Pair with `direct-response-copy` skill for a caption that converts.

## Why these choices

`35mm narrative lens` + `slight haze in the air` are documented Higgsfield
prompt boosters that nudge the model toward a film look without saying the
forbidden word "cinematic" (which dilutes results — see `docs/cookbook.md`
§ "The seven hard rules").
