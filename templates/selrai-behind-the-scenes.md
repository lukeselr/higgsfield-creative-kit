# selrai-behind-the-scenes

**Use when**: you want a candid behind-the-scenes moment — feels iPhone-grade,
not staged, but with film-stock aesthetic baked in. Workhorse for IG Stories.

## Recommended model

- **Photodump** (Higgsfield) — purpose-built for this look.
- Fallback: **Higgsfield Soul 2.0** with `Photodump` style preset.

## Prompt

```text
Candid behind-the-scenes shot, <your-character> caught mid-action while
<doing-the-thing>, slightly handheld feel with mild blur in extremities, 35mm
film grain, mixed practical lighting, off-centre composition with a slice of
the workspace edge intruding from camera-right, no eye contact, natural
expression.
```

## Parameters

- **Aspect ratio**: `9:16` (Stories + Reels) or `4:5` (feed)
- **Quality**: `medium` — high quality looks too polished for BTS
- **Style strength**: `0.6` — keep the rough edges
- **Seed**: don't lock — variation is the point of BTS

## What to fill in

- `<your-character>` — Soul ID name or 8-word description.
- `<doing-the-thing>` — the actual activity. Examples: "wiping down a fresh
  espresso machine", "writing on a whiteboard mid-thought", "stretching a yoga
  mat across the studio floor", "pulling fresh sourdough out of the oven".

## Variations to try once

1. Time of day: `5am pre-dawn blue hour with overhead fluorescent on`
2. Camera angle: `over-the-shoulder, viewer looking past the subject's hand
   into the workspace`
3. Mood: `mid-laugh, head tilted` for warmth, vs. `frustrated, hand on jaw`
   for honesty
4. Detail focus: zoom into hands, or product, or laptop screen — vary across
   a 5-shot BTS series

## Pairing

- Generate 5 variations, drop them into a Reel via your editor with no captions.
  Reads as a real day, not a brand campaign.
- Pair with `humanizer` skill for any caption you write — strips the AI tone.

## Why these choices

`Photodump` is Higgsfield's purpose-built BTS aesthetic — it explicitly does
the slight-handheld-blur look that signals "real moment, not ad". Any prompt
with the word "professional" or "polished" dilutes the look — keep them out.
